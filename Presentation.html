Qubits

    Qubit or quantum bit is the fundamental unit of quantum information processing

  • There is no single standard technology to make quantum computer till 2024.
  • There are various models for making qubit (quantum bit).
  • Each technology has strengths and weaknesses.
  • Most popular models of qubit are mentioned here.

1. Superconducting Qubit

Branch of solid-state quantum computing that
implements superconducting electronic circuits
for making superconducting qubits

127-qubit IBM Quantum Eagle quantum processor
IBM's 5-Qubit Processor

2. Photonic Qubit

Photonic Quantum Computing (PQC) usesÂ an individual particle of light,
known as a photon, as a qubit, the fundamental unit of quantum information.

3. Neutral-Atom Qubit

A neutral atom quantum computer is a modality of
quantum computers built out of Rydberg atoms.

4. Trapped Ion Qubit

Ions, or charged atomic particles, can be confined and suspended
in free space using electromagnetic fields.

5. Quantum dot Qubit

Quantum dots are tiny semiconductor structures that can serve as qubits.
A small semiconductor device that can trap a single electron.

6. NV diamond Qubit

  • One promising area of technological advances in quantum computing is
    the creation of qubits based on nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centres in diamond.
    This means that the processing and storage units of a quantum computer are
    located in specific NV centres in diamonds.
  • An electron spin inside a Nitrogen-Vacancy (NV) center in a diamond lattice is used as qubit

7. Topological Qubit

The qubit is composed of a group of anyons, which do not appear in 3D systems.
It employs quasiparticles in two-dimensional systems, called anyons, whose world
pass around one another to form braids in a three-dimensional spacetime
